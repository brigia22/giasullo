About

My name is Briana. I taught myself HTML when I was a kid and I recently decided to get back into it. I enjoy learning new coding languages but I am most comfortable with HTML, and so my plan is to continue experimenting until I can expand my knowledge into other languages.

This 'About' page is inspired by Derek Sivers in that I intend to update it pretty frequently with information about things that I am currently doing or interested in.


New Project: I recently decided to build a photo collection of snowmen. If you're wondering why... best to just visit the collection!


Reading: Right now I am reading Moby Dick and I am honestly a little surprised at how much I'm enjoying it. Because the book is so prevalent in our culture, I assumed I knew pretty much everything I needed to know about the story at this point in my life. Guy gets really vindictive and hunts a whale and learns a tragic lesson about revenge or something, right? Well so far the book is actually pretty funny, and there is a lot more to the plot and characters than I imagined.

A similar situation occurred to me in October, when I read Mary Shelley's Frankenstein with a small group of friends. We all knew the story of Dr. Frankenstein and his monster but I did not imagine that the story would be so complex and rambling. Spoiler alert: my friends and I were almost unanimous in our hatred for Dr. Frankenstein and his inability to take responsibility for his actions. I'm curious to know if this was the point of his character.


Rereading: Because I get impatient and read multiple books at once, I'm also rereading The Broom of the System by David Foster Wallace. Wallace is my favorite author and this is my favorite book of all time. I highly recommend anything this guy has written; I started with his last novel, The Pale King, but I've heard others recommend starting with his nonfiction essays if you've never read him before.


Yoga: My "yoga journey" if you will began sometime in 2015, when I desperately needed a new exercise to do inside when the weather is too inclement for me to run outside (my main exercise of choice). I started watching YouTube videos by Yoga with Adriene and was hooked pretty quickly. Adriene's videos are easy to follow and she maintains a casual and fun atmosphere, often cracking jokes and leaving her mistakes unedited. It's amazing how much stronger and more flexible I feel from practicing (on average) only about twice a week, for maybe twenty minutes each practice. With a potentially harsh winter ahead of us, I hope to master the Crow Pose by springtime.


Cooking: Pandemic downtime has led me to become a little more adventurous in my cooking habits. With school, work, interning, running, etc. I often felt like cooking was just a hassle that I didn't have time for. Now that I'm working from home, the time that would normally be used up by my bus commute (about forty-five minutes each way) is free for me to experiment with new recipes. My recent successes include:

  • Thanksgiving vegetarian stuffing and mashed potatoes
  • Mushroom casserole
  • Vegetarian chili
  • Italian-style lentil soup
  • White bean and kale soup
  • Vegetarian chicken pot pie (subbed tempeh for chicken)

I am a vegetarian, hence the vegetarian recipes. My biggest failure so far was probably vegetable lasagna, which was more complicated than I anticipated and required a food processor, which I do not own. Lesson learned: Always read all of the recipe's instructions before starting!

Many of the recipes I used can be found in Jennifer Perillo's cookbook, Homemade with Love: Simple Scratch Cooking from In Jennie's Kitchen. The book was given to me as a gift and I was told that it's no longer in print, but a quick Google search provides some options for purchasing it.
